Civil Engineering Department

Welcome to the Civil Engineering Department page of our library management system. Here, you can find information about the books available in our collection, as well as the books currently checked out by students and faculty members.

We are a specialized library that supports the research and learning needs of students, faculty, and staff in the engineering department. Our collection includes a wide range of books, journals, and other resources related to engineering fields such as civil, mechanical, electrical, and more.

Civil engineering books are typically focused on providing a comprehensive understanding of the principles and practices involved in the planning, design, construction, and maintenance of physical infrastructure such as buildings, roads, bridges, tunnels, water supply systems, and sewage systems. These books cover a wide range of topics, from the fundamentals of engineering mechanics and materials science to the application of computer-aided design (CAD) software and advanced analytical techniques. They may also address specific subfields within civil engineering, such as structural engineering, geotechnical engineering, transportation engineering, environmental engineering, and water resources engineering. In addition to theoretical concepts, civil engineering books may provide practical guidance on how to solve real-world problems, including case studies and examples from industry. They may also include code provisions, design standards, and regulatory requirements that are essential for ensuring the safety, sustainability, and efficiency of infrastructure projects. Some popular civil engineering books include "Structural Analysis" by R.C. Hibbeler, "Geotechnical Engineering: Principles and Practices" by Donald P. Coduto, "Transportation Engineering: An Introduction" by C.J. Khisty and B. Kent Lall, "Environmental Engineering: Fundamentals, Sustainability, Design" by James R. Mihelcic and Julie B. Zimmerman, and "Water Resources Engineering" by Larry W. Mays.
